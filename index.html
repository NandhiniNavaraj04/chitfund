What is a Chit Fund?

A chit fund is a savings and investment scheme where a group of people contribute a fixed amount of money every month into a common pool. Each month, one member from the group receives the collected amount, usually decided through a bidding or lottery system. This cycle continues until every member has received the pooled amount once. Chit funds are a popular way to save regularly, access funds quickly in times of need, and earn better returns compared to traditional savings methods — all while promoting financial discipline within a trusted community.

Your Problem Solving

Chit funds help individuals manage financial emergencies and achieve short-term goals without depending on formal credit institutions.

Whether it’s for education, medical needs, or business investment — you get access to lump sums with flexible terms and community trust.

How Does a Chit Fund Work?

Every month, members contribute a fixed amount. The total sum is auctioned among members who bid the lowest for the fund, and the remaining amount is distributed as dividends.

This cycle continues until each member has received the pool once — creating a balance between saving and borrowing.

Benefits of Chit Funds

Chit funds offer flexibility, reliability, and the chance to earn better returns than traditional savings.

  • ✔ Regular saving habit
  • ✔ Quick access to funds
  • ✔ Trusted community management
  • ✔ Potential for good returns
